# Nervous System  

## Peripheral NS  

### Autonomic  

* Enteric  
    
    * Functions: GI Tract  
        * Regulating Gastrointestinal Endocrine Function  
        * Defensive Reactions  
            * Diarrhea  
            * Vomiting  
        * Entero-Enteric Reflexes  
        * Secretions  
            * Pancreatic  
            * Gastric  
        * Fluid Exchange and Blood Flow  
        * Motility  
* Sympathetic  
    * Functions: Fight or Flight  
        * Positive  
            * Pupil dilation and ciliary muscle relaxation: more light and better distance vision  
            * Increased respiration  
            * Dilates bronchioles of the lung  
            * Dilation of blood vessels in muscles, heart, and lungs  
            * Increased heart rate  
            * Increased sweating: thermoregulation  
            * Relaxation of bladder  
            * Piloerection  
            * Stimulates orgasm  
        * Negative  
            * Inhibits peristalsis: decreased digestion  
            * Constricts blood vessels to skin and gut  
            * Decreased salivation: dry mouth  
            * Constricts internal sphincters inhibiting urination and defecation  
            * Inhibits lacrimation or tearing  
        * Chronic activation (stress)  
            * Constipation  
            * Weight dysregulation  
                * Increase consumption of comfort foods  
                * Anorexia  
            * Decrease in fertility  
            * Atherosclerosis  
            * Sleep disorders  
            * Decrease in neurogenesis  
    * Anatomy  
        * Spinal cord: Neurons in lateral gray column  
            * Sympathetic chain of ganglia  
                * Target organs  
* Parasympathetic  
    * Functions: ‘Rest and digest’ or ‘Feed and breed’  
        * Increased peristalsis: Promotes digestion  
        * Pupillary constriction and ciliary muscle constriction: Promotes near vision  
        * Relaxes internal sphincters: Promotes urination and defecation  
        * Promotes erection and sexual arousal  
        * Constricts bronchioles of the lung  
        * Increases blood flow to the gut and skin, and decreases blood flow to the muscles, lungs, and heart  
        * Decreased heart rate  
        * Decreased rate of respiration  
    * Anatomy  
        * Cell bodies in brainstem nuclei of cranial nerves (III, VII, IX, X)  
            * Ganglia for Cranial Nerves  
                * IX: Mouth  
                * X: Internal organs  
                * VII: Nose  
                * III: Cilary muscle eye  
        * Cell bodes in sacral aspect of spinal cord at levels S2, S3, S4  
            * Parasympathetic ganglia  
                * Target organs
