Hominin brain expansion

A compact teaching figure for the claim that hominin brain size did not merely rise with body size. The main panel shows endocranial volume through time; the inset contrasts observed brain enlargement with a body-size-only allometric expectation.

Teaching caveat. The fossil record is sparse and taxonomic assignments are debated. This figure uses representative anchors and deliberately emphasizes the human-through-line, while using a few gray side branches to keep students from reading the plot as a simple ladder.

Caption. Endocranial volume increased slowly among earlier hominins and then rose more steeply during the early history of Homo. DeSilva et al. (2021) report positive rate changes near 2.1 and 1.5 Ma; newer phylogenetic work emphasizes accelerating relative brain-size increase through time and cautions that within-species trends, body size, and taxonomy must be separated.

Source notes. Core teaching values are drawn from museum-level summaries and recent reviews: early hominins around 350 ml; Au. afarensis around 450 cc; H. habilis around 610 cc; H. ergaster / early H. erectus around 860 cc; H. heidelbergensis around 1250 cc; recent H. sapiens around 1350 cc. The allometric line is not a fitted fossil regression; it is a visual baseline anchored to Au. afarensis with a slope near 0.75, used to ask how far body size alone would move the brain.
